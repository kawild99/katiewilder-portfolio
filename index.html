About Me

I discovered my passion for coding in 2016 when I took a computer science course in high school. I enjoyed the class so much that I started looking for ways to teach others coding. I was hired as a Teaching Assistant for Thinking Feet, a company that helps K-5 students receive enrichment in Computer Programming. As a TA, I helped advise a small group of children on the basics of coding using MIT’s Scratch platform. I held that position until the summer of 2017, when I left for college.

I graduated from UNC Chapel Hill in May 2021 as a Business Administration major and an Information Systems minor. I have a passion for both Finance and Technology, but ultimately decided to take a post-college job in the Business world. Although I thoroughly enjoy Business, I haven’t been able to shake the love I have for coding. I decided to enroll in the UNC Coding Bootcamp, a 6-month journey that teaches a rigorous curriculum which covers a full stack: HTML5, CSS3, JavaScript, jQuery, Express.js, React.js, Node.js, progressive web apps, agile methodology, computer science, database theory, MongoDB, MySQL, Git, and more. This class has helped me develop my coding skills more than I ever thought possible! All works represented on this website are projects that I worked on during the UNC Coding Bootcamp.
